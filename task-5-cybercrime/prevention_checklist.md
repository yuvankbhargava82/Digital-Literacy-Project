 **Stay Safe Online** checklist:

---

###  Account & Password Security

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.** Never reuse passwords across your college email, social media, and banking apps. Use a free password manager like Bitwarden to generate and store them.

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts.** Turn it on for Gmail, Instagram, and your banking/UPI apps. Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator) — not just SMS, as SIM-swap fraud is common in India.

---

###  UPI & Financial Safety

- [ ] **Never enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money.** Your PIN is only needed to *send* money. If someone asks for your PIN or sends a "collect request" saying it will transfer money *to* you — it's a scam. Decline immediately and block the contact.

- [ ] **Verify the recipient's name before every UPI transfer.** Always confirm the name the app shows after you enter a UPI ID. Fraudsters use IDs like *refund@okaxis* or *support@ybl* to appear legitimate. Also set a daily transaction limit in your bank app and enable SMS/email alerts for all payments.

---

### Phishing & Scam Awareness

- [ ] **Never click links in WhatsApp, SMS, or emails from unknown senders.** Fake scholarship offers, "earn ₹500/task" part-time job scams, and fake KYC update alerts are rampant. Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself.

- [ ] **Never call customer care numbers found on Google.** Fraudsters pay to rank fake helpline numbers above real ones. Always use the number printed on your debit card or inside the official app. If "support staff" asks you to install AnyDesk or TeamViewer, hang up — it's a scam.

---

###  Social Media & Privacy

- [ ] **Set all social media profiles to private.** Avoid publicly sharing your location, hostel address, college ID, date of birth, or Aadhaar details. Cybercriminals mine open profiles to craft targeted attacks against you.

- [ ] **Beware of fake friendship/romance accounts.** If someone you met only online asks for money, gift cards, or intimate photos — stop all contact. Sextortion (threats to share manipulated images unless you pay) is a serious and growing crime. Do not pay; report it immediately.

---

###  Device & Network Security

- [ ] **Never do banking or UPI transactions on public Wi-Fi.** Hostel common room networks, café Wi-Fi, and open college networks can be monitored. If unavoidable, use a reputable free VPN like Proton VPN to encrypt your connection.

- [ ] **Only install apps from the official Play Store or App Store.** Never install APK files shared via WhatsApp or Telegram — this is the most common way malware spreads on Indian Android phones. Keep your OS and apps updated for the latest security patches.

---

###  If You're a Victim — Report Immediately

Time is critical. Reporting within the **first hour** significantly increases the chance of recovering money and catching the criminal.
