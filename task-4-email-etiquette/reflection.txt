One real situation where poor digital communication caused a serious problem was during the COVID-19 lockdown, when many schools shifted to online classes. In some rural and small-town areas, schools sent important updates only through email or school apps without explaining how to access them. Many parents and students were unfamiliar with these platforms, so they missed class schedules, exam dates, and assignment submissions. This led to confusion, stress, and poor academic performance for many students.
This situation could have been handled better by using multiple communication methods such as SMS, WhatsApp messages, and phone calls in local languages. Schools could also have provided short guidance videos or step-by-step instructions to help parents and students understand digital tools. Clear, simple, and inclusive communication would have reduced confusion and ensured equal access to education.
